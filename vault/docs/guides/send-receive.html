Sending & Receiving Canton Assets

A practical guide to moving Canton assets in and out of your Jutis wallet. Covers receive addresses, send flow, confirmation checklist, and transaction history.

info

Canton live network pending

This guide describes the full send/receive experience. Canton mainnet integration is pending — for now, Jutis is fully functional as a local wallet. Live Canton asset transfers will be enabled following testnet activation and final integration testing.

Receive assets

To receive Canton assets, share your Canton address with the sender. Your address is a long string starting with canton1. It is not a secret — you can share it freely to receive funds.

  1. Open the Jutis extension and navigate to the Wallet or Addresses section
  2. Click the Copy button next to your Canton address
  3. Share the address with the sender (email, message, or QR code)
  4. Once the transaction is confirmed on the Canton network, the assets appear in your wallet balance

Your address never changes

Unlike session-based identifiers, your Canton address is permanent. You can use the same address for all future deposits. There is no concept of a "new address per transaction" in the Canton model.

QR code option

Jutis can display your address as a QR code for easy scanning. Click the QR icon next to your address in the extension. Useful for mobile-to-desktop transfers.

Send assets

Sending Canton assets requires three pieces of information and one confirmation step inside the Jutis extension.

  1. Recipient address — A Canton address starting with canton1. Double-check before pasting.
  2. Amount — The quantity of CANT (or other Canton-native asset) to send.
  3. Network / fee — Canton network fee, shown automatically before you confirm.
  4. Extension confirmation — Jutis shows a popup with all details. Approve to sign and broadcast.

Confirm the recipient address

Canton addresses are long and difficult to read manually. Always copy and paste rather than typing by hand. A single incorrect character sends assets to the wrong address — and Canton transactions are irreversible.

warning

Irreversible transactions

Canton blockchain transactions cannot be reversed, cancelled, or refunded. If you send to the wrong address, the assets are permanently lost. Jutis has no ability to reverse or recover transactions.

Before confirming any send, verify:

  • The full recipient address starts with canton1
  • The address is from a source you trust (not copy-pasted from an unverified message)
  • The amount shown matches what you intended to send
  • You are connected to the correct Canton network (mainnet vs testnet)

Review network and fees

Every Canton transaction consumes a small network fee, paid in CANT. Jutis displays the fee before you approve the transaction — there are no hidden fees.

Network fee

The fee goes to Canton validators for processing your transaction. It varies based on network congestion. Jutis estimates the fee and shows it clearly before you confirm.

Total amount

The amount you enter plus the network fee is the total deducted from your balance. Jutis shows both separately so you know exactly what the recipient receives vs. what you pay.

Transaction history

Jutis records all transactions initiated from your wallet. You can view the history in the History tab of the dashboard. Each entry shows:

  • Type — Send or receive
  • Amount — CANT amount transferred
  • Status — Completed, pending, or failed
  • Counterparty — Truncated Canton address
  • Time — Timestamp of the transaction
  • Tx hash — Link to Canton block explorer (when live)

Safety warnings

warning

Only send on the Canton network

Jutis supports Canton assets exclusively. Do not send ETH, BTC, USDC on Ethereum, or any other non-Canton asset to your Canton address — those assets are permanently irrecoverable if sent to the wrong network.

warning

Test before sending large amounts

For new wallets or new recipients, always send a small test amount first. Confirm the recipient receives it before transferring larger balances.

info

Jutis staff will never ask for your recovery phrase

No Jutis team member, admin, or support agent will ever ask for your recovery phrase, private key, or wallet password. Anyone who does is attempting to steal your assets.

Troubleshooting

Transaction not arriving

First check the transaction status in the History tab. If it shows as pending, the Canton network may be congested. If it shows as failed, the transaction was never broadcast — check your balance to confirm no funds were deducted.

Wrong network warning

If you see a warning that the recipient is on a different network, stop the transaction immediately. Sending Canton assets to a non-Canton address (e.g., Ethereum address) will result in permanent asset loss.

Insufficient balance

Jutis shows your available and locked balances. Canton transactions require a small fee in addition to the transfer amount. Ensure your balance covers both the amount and the estimated network fee.

Extension not responding

If the Jutis extension doesn't open when you click the send button, check that the extension is pinned and active in your browser toolbar. Try refreshing the page. If issues persist, the extension may need to be reinstalled — your wallet and assets are safe as long as your recovery phrase is stored offline.
