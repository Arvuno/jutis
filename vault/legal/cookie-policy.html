1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet, or other internet-enabled device) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give website owners useful information about how visitors use their sites.

Cookies may be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third parties who provide services to that website ("third-party cookies").

In addition to cookies, we may use similar tracking technologies, including:

  • Local Storage: Data stored in your browser's local storage that persists even after you close your browser.
  • Session Storage: Data stored for the duration of your browser session that is deleted when you close your browser.
  • Pixels and Web Beacons: Small image files or code snippets that help us understand how you interact with our emails or certain pages.

This Cookie Policy uses the term "cookies" to refer to all these technologies unless otherwise specified.

Essential cookies are necessary for the Service to function properly. They enable core features such as security, authentication, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, certain parts of the Service would not work.

Because these cookies are required for the Service to function, they cannot be disabled. They are not used to track your browsing activity or build a profile of you.

Examples of essential cookies we use include:

  • Security tokens: Verify your identity and protect against unauthorized access

  • Wallet connection state: Remember your connected wallet address during your session

5. Preference Cookies

Preference cookies (also called "functionality cookies") enable the Service to remember choices you make and provide enhanced, personalized features. These cookies enhance your experience by remembering settings such as your language preference, region, or whether you have seen certain notifications.

Preference cookies do not track your browsing activity on other websites. They are used solely for the purpose of improving your experience on the Service.

Examples of preference cookies we may use include:

  • Language preferences: Remember your selected language for future visits
  • Theme preferences: Remember if you have selected light or dark mode
  • Notification preferences: Remember which notifications you have dismissed
  • Sidebar state: Remember if you have collapsed or expanded certain interface elements

Preference cookies have a defined lifespan. Some may persist across sessions (e.g., language preference), while others are deleted when you close your browser (e.g., sidebar state).

7. Managing Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies and similar tracking technologies:

7.1 Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from all or certain websites
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Clear all cookies when you close your browser

The steps to manage cookies vary by browser. Consult your browser's help documentation for specific instructions. Please note that blocking essential cookies may prevent certain features of the Service from working properly.

7.2 Browser Extensions

Various browser extensions are available that can help you manage cookies and tracking preferences, including tools that can block specific tracking technologies. We do not endorse or recommend any specific extension.

7.3 Mobile Device Controls

If you access the Service from a mobile device, you may be able to manage tracking preferences through your device settings or within the app (if applicable).
